About Jan Baer

Software Craftsman & Passionate Photographer

With over 30 years of tinkering and more than 25 in the industry professionally, my path through software development has been anything but linear — and I wouldn’t have it any other way.

I started in the mid-90s with Visual Basic, moved through Java, and then spent well over a decade deep in C# and .NET — building everything from medical software to banking systems. Eventually JavaScript and Node.js pulled me toward the web, and today TypeScript, Go, Svelte, and Astro are the tools I reach for most.

Around 2010 I made a deliberate shift toward infrastructure and the cloud. For the past 8+ years I’ve been operating production systems as both developer and DevOps engineer — managing 50+ Linux servers, two Kubernetes clusters running K3s, MongoDB replica sets, and a full Infrastructure-as-Code stack with Ansible and Terraform. In 2018 I also moved my desktop to NixOS, joining my servers. Declarative, reproducible, and honest — it changed how I think about systems entirely.

Building side projects has always been how I learn and scratch my own itches. Over the years I’ve built IDE plugins, knowledge base tools, cloud bookmarking apps, wikis, and more — each one solving a real problem I had at the time. See the Projects page for the full history.

When I’m not at the keyboard, I’m behind a camera — landscapes, nature, aerial drone shots, and long exposures. The patience and attention to detail that good photography demands aren’t so different from good software. Both reward slowing down.
